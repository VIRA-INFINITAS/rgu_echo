LGBTQIA+

Supporting diversity and inclusion

We live in a time when LGBTQIA+ rights are advancing and identities are celebrated by many, however some LGBTQIA+ students might face unique challenges at university due to societal attitudes, discrimination, and a lack of understanding or support.

RGU is committed to equality and diversity, and is working to create an inclusive environment for everyone. There are staff and student equality champions for each protected characteristic under the equality act, and we would encourage you to reach out the them if you have anything you'd like to discuss around sexuality or gender identity.

Recognising a Problem

Challenges may include:

  • Discrimination and Harassment: Facing discrimination, microaggressions, or outright harassment from peers, faculty, or staff based on sexual orientation, gender identity, or expression.
  • Lack of Representation: Feeling a lack of representation or inclusivity in the curriculum, course materials, or university policies, which can lead to feelings of exclusion.
  • Mental Health Concerns: Dealing with mental health issues such as anxiety, depression, or low self-esteem due to the stress of navigating an environment that may not be fully accepting or supportive.
  • Isolation and Loneliness: Struggling with a sense of isolation or loneliness, especially if they don't have a supportive community or if they haven't come out and fear rejection.
  • Accessing Support Services: Difficulty finding or accessing adequate support services, including counseling or LGBTQIA+ support groups, that understand their specific needs.
  • Housing and Safety Concerns: Facing challenges related to housing, such as feeling unsafe in student accommodation or encountering issues with flatmates due to their sexual orientation or gender identity.
  • Legal and Policy Issues: Dealing with legal or policy challenges related to gender markers, name changes, or accessing gender-affirming healthcare services on campus.
  • Navigating Identity Exploration: Struggling with the process of exploring and understanding their identity in an environment that may not fully embrace or validate diverse gender identities and sexual orientations.
