Other Addictive Behaviours

Understanding digital and behavioral addictions

Recognising a Problem

Excessive gaming, shopping, pornography use, internet use, social media or other excessive behaviours can become problematic. Although people who experience problems with these types of addictive behaviours may not experience any physical symptoms, they may encounter negative consequences if the pattern of behaviour impedes their daily functioning. If these behaviours interfere with daily life, it's time to address the issue.

Impact on Studies: Digital addictions can negatively impact focus and time management, affecting academic performance. Be aware of how these activities may be influencing your studies.

Concern for Others: Approach friends or family members with empathy if you notice excessive digital engagement. Encourage them to evaluate their behaviours and support them in seeking professional help when necessary.

Recognising a Problem

  • Is the behaviour interfering with your commitments or responsibilities?
  • Does the behaviour initially provide a sensation of relief or reward but afterwards leave you feeling guilty or remorseful?
  • Are you spending an increasing amount of time on the behaviour at the expense of other elements of your life (e.g. seeing friends/family, attending university/work, engaging in hobbies/interests)?
  • Has anyone expressed concern about your engagement with the behaviour?
  • Has engaging in the behaviour resulted in arguments/relationship difficulties?
  • Do you regularly lose sleep or turn night into day in order to engage in the behaviour?

If the answer to any of these questions is 'yes' then this may indicate that your engagement with this behaviour is problematic and it may be time to seek help.

What You Can Do

Seek professional guidance and explore self-help strategies like setting digital boundaries, finding alternative activities, and building a support system.
