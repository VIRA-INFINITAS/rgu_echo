Revision/Exams

Managing exam stress and staying focused

Experiencing stress during exam and revision periods is a common response to academic pressure. A moderate level of stress can be motivating and help you stay focussed, but excessive or prolonged stress can negatively impact both your mental and physical health. You may have difficulty concentrating, feel irritable, have headaches, or changes in appetite and sleep patterns. While it's normal to feel anxious in the lead-up to exams, it's important to develop healthy coping strategies such as taking regular breaks, staying physically active, and maintaining a balanced routine. If stress becomes overwhelming or persistent, it may be a sign of an underlying issue such as anxiety or depression, and it is important to seek support to help you manage your wellbeing.
