My Personal Tutor

Your dedicated support for academic and personal development

What is a Personal Tutor?

The University works hard to create an environment in which all students are supported to further their personal, social and academic development. The Personal Tutor is a key support person for students. Personal Tutors are members of staff from the student's own School who provide advice and guidance on a range of personal and academic matters. They do this by meeting with the student, listening to any concerns the student has, and then either working with the student to find a solution or referring the student onto other support services in the University, if appropriate. The student's own School assigns a Personal Tutor to each student who is then provided with the name and contact details of the Personal Tutor.

How do I contact my Personal Tutor?

Some Schools put this information on to your Moodle profile which you can access here CampusMoodle. Others will have this information on your Course page in Moodle, and you will also have been emailed this information at the start of term. You can also contact your school administrator if you can't find the information. If they are unavailable or you wish to speak to someone else, your Course Leader should be able to help you. And you can always make use of the support from Student Life.

Regular Meetings

Schedule regular check-ins with your Personal Tutor throughout the academic year.

Open Communication

Feel free to discuss academic progress, personal challenges, or career goals.

Referrals

Your tutor can connect you with specialist support services when needed.
